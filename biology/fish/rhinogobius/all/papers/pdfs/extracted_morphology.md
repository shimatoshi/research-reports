### Head Sensory Canals
- **POC (posterior oculoscapular canal):** Present, with two terminal pores K' and L'
- **POP (preopercular canal):** Present, with three pores M', N, and O'
- Anterior oculoscapular canal: nasal extension with terminal pore B'; anterior interorbital sections separated, with paired pore C and single pore D; pore E present; lateral section with pores F and H'

### D1 Shape in Males
- Falcate; second spine longest but not filamentous; when adpressed, rear tip extending to base of fourth branched ray of second dorsal fin

### Squamation
- Body scales moderately large and ctenoid posteriorly, small and cycloid anteriorly
- Base of pectoral fin with some small cycloid scales (scaled)
- Belly with small cycloid scales
- A small cycloid scale between genital papillae and origin of anal fin
- Anterior part of predorsal area naked
- Predorsal squamation with trifurcate anterior edge, middle series extending beyond above canal pore H'
- Head and prepelvic area naked

### Coloration
- **Cheek:** With 10 or more small pale-sky spots (male); fewer, pale spots (female)
- **Pectoral fin base:** White with two deep yellowish pink crescent-shaped marks
- **Caudal fin base:** Lacking distinct rows of dark dots; a pair of diagonal dark-brown blotches forming incomplete bold C-shape in young and females
- **Caudal fin:** No distinct rows of dark dots
- **Body:** Ground color light bluish gray; cheek with deep pink network pattern; snout with vivid red oblique stripe; nape with longitudinal vivid-red stripes
- **Fins:** Vertical fins with deep pink rays; distal margins white

### Max SL
- 77.5 mm SL (female paratype); holotype male 67.2 mm SL

### Life History Type
- Amphidromous; sometimes landlocked in freshwater reservoirs
- Distribution: middle reaches or mountain torrents of freshwater streams in Japan (western Hokkaido southward to southern Kyushu) and Cheju Island, Korea

### Head Sensory Canals
- **POC (posterior oculoscapular canal):** Usually absent (9 of 10 specimens lack it; 1 has it)
- **POP (preopercular canal):** Usually present; 3 specimens have pores M', N, and O'; 4 (including holotype) lack intermediate pore N; 1 specimen lacks the canal entirely
- Anterior oculoscapular canal: considerable variation; 8 specimens have nasal extension with B', separated interorbital sections with C and D, pore E, lateral section with F and H'; some specimens show reduced canal development

### D1 Shape in Males
- Low, trapezoid or half oval; third spine longest; not filamentous; when adpressed, posterior end usually not extending to origin of second dorsal fin

### Squamation
- Body scales small ctenoid anteriorly, moderately large ctenoid posteriorly
- Basal part of caudal fin with small cycloid scales
- Anterodorsal part of body before diagonal from D1 origin to P insertion with small scales
- Anterior part of predorsal area naked
- Predorsal squamation with trifurcate anterior edge, anterior extensions extending beyond above canal pore H'
- Head naked
- Lateral and ventral sides of belly with ctenoid and small cycloid scales, respectively
- Pelvic-fin axil naked; belly scales not extending anteriorly to pelvic-fin insertion
- Pectoral fin base and prepelvic areas with 0-4 and 0-17 small cycloid embedded scales, respectively

### Coloration
- **Cheek:** Several vague, irregularly-shaped yellow spots (male); no yellow markings (female)
- **Pectoral fin base:** Dorsalmost with a triangular bright blue marking
- **Caudal fin base:** "<"-shaped grayish brown blotch (male); "<"-shaped grayish brown blotch (female)
- **Caudal fin:** Central part with four red-purple vertical lines (male); 1-7 reddish gray vertical lines or rows of dots (female)
- **Body:** Ground color yellowish gray; midlateral body with broad dull blue stripe; belly whitish with yellow and blue
- **D1:** Pale yellow turning to dull purple distally, with broad pale-yellow anterodorsal margins; no dark large circle or quadrangle markings
- **Anal fin:** Grayish with broad midlateral orange stripe and white distal margin
- **Caudal fin:** Lower half reddish orange in males

### Max SL
- 40.0 mm SL (largest paratype); body relatively short and small (reaching up to 40 mm SL)

### Life History Type
- Non-diadromous (landlocked); found in shallow freshwater areas with mud bottoms and aquatic vegetation (ponds, marshes, reservoirs, canals, creeks)
- Distribution: inland waters in temperate Japan along Seto Inland Sea, Osaka Bay, Kii Channel; also in Tokai District (possibly introduced)

- Low, trapezoid or "shogi-piece" shaped; third spine longest; not filamentous; when adpressed, third-spine tip not extending to origin of second dorsal fin in both sexes

### Squamation
- Body scales small ctenoid anteriorly, moderately large ctenoid posteriorly
- Basal part of caudal fin with small cycloid scales
- Anterodorsal part of body before diagonal from middle of D1 base to P insertion with small scales
- Anterior part of predorsal area naked
- Predorsal squamation with trifurcate anterior edge, extending anteriorly beyond above through middle of opercle
- Head naked
- Lateral and ventral sides of belly with ctenoid and small cycloid scales, respectively
- Pelvic-fin axil naked; scaly area of belly usually extending anteriorly to pelvic-fin insertion
- Pectoral fin base and prepelvic areas usually with some small cycloid scales (0-4 in preventral midline)

### Coloration
- **Cheek:** Grayish; dorsal parts of cheek and operculum with several irregular-shaped short dull orange lines or spots
- **Pectoral fin base:** Large oblong black marking
- **Caudal fin base:** Ventral part with two black blotches (male); "<"-shaped grayish brown blotch (female)
- **Caudal fin:** Central part with two or three vertical rows of gray dots (male); rows of dots in females
- **D1:** Row of narrow, transversely-elongate violet blotches along spines (single longitudinal row of vertically-elongate dark markings)
- **Anal fin:** Light yellowish orange with narrow white lower margin
- **Body:** Ground color yellowish gray; six saddle-like blotches on dorsum
- **Lower half of caudal fin:** Without reddish orange coloration (unlike R. tyoni)

### Max SL
- 39.5 mm SL (largest paratype); body relatively short and small (reaching up to 40 mm SL)

### Life History Type
- Non-diadromous (landlocked); found in shallow freshwater areas with mud bottoms and aquatic vegetation (ponds, marshes, reservoirs, canals, creeks)
- Distribution: Tokai District of temperate Japan (Aichi, Mie, Gifu, and Shizuoka prefectures)

### Head Sensory Canals
- **POC (posterior oculoscapular canal):** Present, with two terminal pores (theta and iota)
- **POP (preopercular canal):** Present, with three pores (gamma, delta, and epsilon)
- Anterior oculoscapular canal: nasal extension with terminal pore sigma; anterior interorbital sections separated with paired pore lambda; single pore kappa; pore omega near posterior edge of eye; lateral section with corner pore alpha and terminal pore rho

### D1 Shape in Males
- Second spinous ray always longest, especially in adult males; rear tip of D1 extending to base of 2nd to 6th branched rays of D2 when depressed in males (elongate/falcate)

### Squamation
- Body with moderately large ctenoid scales on posterior body and smaller cycloid scales on anterior half
- Belly with tiny cycloid scales
- Anterior predorsal region naked
- Predorsal squamation with trifurcate anterior edge, middle series extending to vertical through upper origin of gill-opening in males, further forward around midline vertical of opercle in females
- Head and prepelvic region naked

### Coloration
- **Cheek:** 6-10 orange red spots in males; fewer than 5 blackish brown to brownish red spots in females
- **Pectoral fin base:** Horizontal deep black diaper (bar-shaped) mark in upper half
- **Caudal fin base:** A pair of dark brown bars vertically oriented, more conspicuous and larger in females
- **Caudal fin:** 3-5 vertical rows of stripes or spots in middle region
- **Snout:** A pair of broad deep red stripes united on snout tip
- **Nape:** Several longitudinal reddish brown to deep brown stripes
- **Body:** No saddle-shaped patches of dark dots on dorsal side; intermittent dark dotted rows along middle axis
- **D1:** Grayish with yellowish anterior region on upper half in male; pale with crescent grayish black region after spines in female
- **Ventral body:** Yellowish white

### Max SL
- 75.2 mm SL (holotype male); largest paratype 75.2 mm SL

### Life History Type
- Amphidromous (small-egg type; ripe oocyte diameter 0.69-0.88 mm, average 0.77 mm)
- Endemic to Bonin Islands (Ogasawara Group), Japan: Anijima, Chichijima, Hahajima and Otoutojima Islands

- Body and caudal peduncle elongate: body depth 13.8-14.5% SL at anal fin origin; caudal peduncle depth 11.4-12.2% SL
- Fifth pelvic-fin segmented ray usually divided into five branches at most proximal segmenting point
- Six spines in first dorsal fin

- Anterodorsal part of body before diagonal from D1-D2 area to P insertion with cycloid scales
- Belly with small cycloid scales; naked area around ventral midline
- Pectoral-fin base naked
- Prepelvic area naked
- Predorsal squamation with trifurcate anterior edge

- **Cheek:** Many small bright orange dots (male); indistinct, fewer (female)
- **Pectoral fin base:** Distinct dull orange oval spot
- **Caudal fin base:** A pair of vertically-arranged, rounded or rectangular black blotches (female); no described blotch in male
- **Caudal fin:** Four vertical rows of bright orange dots (male); without barred pattern (female)
- **Temporal region:** Two orange stripes reaching posteriorly to or around vertical through D2 origin
- **Body:** Ground color baby blue (alive); dorsum with four saddle-like black blotches; seven large black blotches on midlateral body
- **D1:** No dark spots described

- 66.0 mm SL (paratype male); holotype male 60.0 mm SL

### Life History Type
- Appears to be amphidromous, based on small-sized eggs; one landlocked population known (O'omija-gawa River, Iriomote-jima Island)
- Distribution: upper reaches of swift freshwater streams in montane areas of Ishigaki-jima and Iriomote-jima islands, Yaeyama Group of the Ryukyu Islands, Japan

- Near falcate shape, pentagon (becoming slightly narrower distally) or semioval, higher than D2; usually second or third spine longest; usually not filamentous, or distal tip slightly filamentous; posterior tip extending between front of origin and base of third segmented ray of D2 when adpressed

### Key Diagnostic Features
- Anteriormost two pterygiophores of D2 mounted over neural spine of 10th (sometimes 9th and 10th) vertebra
- Fifth segmented pelvic-fin ray divided into 3-4 (usually four) branches
- Yellow-colored body in freshly-collected; no dark spot on D1

### Squamation
- Body largely ctenoid, becoming smaller anteriorly
- Anterior edge of ctenoid scale areas reaching to: a vertical between behind pectoral-fin axilla and D2 origin (lateral); D2 base (dorsal); A base (ventral)
- Small cycloid scales on anterodorsal part before ctenoid area
- Predorsal squamation with trifurcate anterior edge; mid-anterior extension to between H' and L'; lateral extensions to POC
- Pectoral-fin base usually naked or with 1-2 small cycloid scales
- Prepelvic area usually naked or with some small cycloid scales
- Belly with small cycloid scales; ventral midline of belly usually naked or with some small cycloid scales

### Coloration
- **Cheek:** Usually with densely-arranged many small reddish orange dots (male); fewer (female)
- **Pectoral fin base:** Distinct reddish orange bow-shaped mark, and 1-2 vertical rows of reddish orange spots behind it
- **Caudal fin base:** A pair of short, vertically aligned, rod-shaped deep reddish orange mark (female)
- **Caudal fin:** 4-14 vertical rows of reddish orange or pale orange spots, or forming zigzag bands (male); 3-10 vertical rows of pale pink spots or zigzag bands (female)
- **Body:** Ground color usually reddish yellow; belly pale yellow with greenish sides
- **D1:** No dark spot; anterodorsal part bright yellow

### Max SL
- 70.5 mm SL (paratype male); holotype male 65.9 mm SL

### Life History Type
- Landlocked (freshwater resident); found in upper reaches of freshwater streams above waterfalls in montane areas
- Distribution: Iriomote-jima Island only, Yaeyama Group of the Ryukyu Islands, Japan
